# Build-Up Lab Product Direction

## One-Line Concept

Build-Up Lab is an interactive world-stage football tactics challenge where the player solves match-defining build-up moments: read the press, find the free man, and escape pressure in limited actions.

## Why Pivot

The previous Pass Master prototype proved that the base canvas loop works: pass selection, interception, level progression, star scoring, undo, sound, and simple defensive movement are already present.

The weak point was market positioning. A generic soccer passing puzzle is easy to understand, but it does not create a strong reason to share, replay, or pay attention. A build-up and press-escape puzzle is more specific, more football-native, and more differentiated.

## Target Audience

- Football tactics fans who watch analysis content.
- Football Manager / tactical-board users who enjoy shape and movement.
- Amateur coaches or players who want quick tactical scenarios.
- Casual football fans if the first levels are visual and simple enough.

This is not a mass-market arcade game first. It should start as a sharp niche product with a clear hook, then package that hook with a tournament-style presentation broad enough for casual football fans.

## Product Promise

The player should feel:

- "I can see the opponent's press."
- "I found the free man."
- "That third-man route was satisfying."
- "I want to try one more shape."
- "This feels like a decisive match moment."

## Core Loop

The current loop needs reform. A static "find the correct pass" puzzle is not fun enough on its own.

New target loop:

1. Scan the opponent's pressure.
2. Create or protect a passing angle.
3. Commit a pass, carry, or tactical action.
4. Watch the press react and pressure rise.
5. Exploit the opened space before the press collapses.
6. Get rated on concept execution, pressure control, efficiency, and risk.

The game should feel like surviving and manipulating pressure, not only selecting a legal line.

## MVP Scope

The first version should be a build-up puzzle framed as a world-stage match moment, not a full football simulator.

Include:

- 2D top-down pitch.
- 6-8 attacking players.
- 4-6 pressing defenders.
- Fixed scenario-based levels.
- Limited actions per level.
- Pressing lanes, cover shadows, and target escape zones.
- Tactical actions such as bounce pass, third-man run, switch play, and drop pivot.
- Match-moment framing: minute, score state, formation vs formation, and shareable route summary.

Exclude for now:

- Full 11v11 simulation.
- Real-time dribbling.
- Physics-heavy ball movement.
- Online multiplayer.
- Licensed teams or player names.
- Complex stamina, morale, or player attributes.
- Official tournament, team, or player branding unless licensed.

## Differentiation

Most football games focus on action, management, or trivia. This project should focus on the moment that tactics fans talk about constantly: breaking the press.

The hook:

> Can you beat a 4-4-2 high press in three actions?

That is clearer and stronger than:

> Pass the ball to avoid defenders.

## Success Criteria For Prototype

Within the next prototype, the game should prove:

- A new player understands the objective within 30 seconds.
- A new player understands the pressure meter within 10 seconds.
- A football-aware player recognizes the tactical idea immediately.
- At least 20 test players can complete the first 5 levels without explanation.
- At least 30% of testers voluntarily play 5 or more levels.
- The game produces shareable puzzle moments: "try solving this press."
- A casual football fan understands that each scenario is a high-stakes match moment, not a generic drill.
- Failed attempts feel close enough that players retry voluntarily.

## Monetization Direction

Do not add monetization before the core puzzle is proven.

Potential later paths:

- Free daily challenge with ads.
- Paid level packs: Build-Up vs 4-4-2, vs 4-3-3, vs man-to-man press.
- Tournament-style challenge packs: Group Stage Patterns, Knockout Press Traps, Final Minutes.
- Coach/education mode with editable scenarios.
- Mobile web release first, then app store wrapper if retention is real.
